The Comparison between these two graphs show that they follow a similar pattern.

As given here, the Standard Normal Distribution defines that 68.3\% of values in the set lie in distance of 1 standard deviation(1$\sigma$) from the mean. 
The Standard Normal Distribution also defines that 95.4\% of values in the set lie in distance of 2 standard deviations(2$\sigma$) from the mean. 


The $\mu$-1$\sigma$ is at 21.9 and 36.59 for the Normal Distribution for the given dataset. Such that,we can say that 68.3\% of data lie between 21.9 and 36.59 boundaries.  
The $\mu$-2$\sigma$ is at 14.56 and 43.94 for the Normal Distribution for the given dataset. Such that,we can say that 95.4\% of data lie between 14.56 and 43.94 boundaries.
